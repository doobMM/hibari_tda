## 2. 일본 사카모토 재단 공식 이메일 (영어)

**받는 사람:** Sakamoto Foundation 공식 연락처 (info@ 또는 contact@)
**제목:** Research Proposal: Topological Analysis of Ryuichi Sakamoto's "hibari"

```
Dear Sakamoto Foundation,

My name is Min-ju Kim, and I am a Korean student deeply influenced by the work of
Ryuichi Sakamoto, particularly his minimalist composition "hibari." I am writing to
share a research project I have completed analyzing this piece through the lens of
topological data analysis (TDA), and to ask whether the foundation might be interested
in seeing the results.

== Background ==

I have long been fascinated by how "hibari," through its minimal repeating modules,
manages to evoke vast emotional landscapes. The mathematical question I asked was:
What is the underlying topological structure of this piece, and can we preserve that
structure while generating new variations?

== Research Summary ==

I designed a pipeline based on Persistent Homology, a tool from algebraic topology,
to extract the cyclic structures hidden in "hibari." The piece is converted into a
music network where notes are vertices and weighted edges represent their adjacency
in the score. I then introduced several musical distance functions—including the
Tonnetz lattice from neo-Riemannian theory—to capture harmonic relationships beyond
simple frequency statistics.

Key contributions of the research include:

1. A weighting system that separates intra-instrument flow (melody) and inter-instrument
   interaction (harmony), allowing the analysis to scan a spectrum from melodic to
   harmonic interpretation.

2. The use of Tonnetz-based distance, which improved the structural similarity (measured
   by Jensen-Shannon divergence on pitch distributions) by a factor of 18 compared to
   purely statistical distances.

3. A module-level deep learning approach that respects "hibari"'s 4-bar structure
   (33 modules of 32 eighth notes each), which resolved the failure of sequence models
   on the full 1088-step sequence and improved LSTM performance by a factor of 42.

4. An interactive dashboard where users can edit the topological "seed" (an overlap
   matrix representing which structural cycles are active at each moment) and
   generate new music in real time.

The best configuration (Tonnetz distance with α=0.3, FC neural network, module-level
learning) achieves a Jensen-Shannon divergence of 0.002 between the original and
generated pitch distributions—essentially preserving the harmonic essence of "hibari"
while producing genuinely new music.

== About the Author ==

I designed the methodology, the musical interpretation, and the research direction.
The code implementation was done with the assistance of Anthropic's Claude AI. This
collaboration between human musical intuition and AI implementation power was, in
itself, a meaningful experience for me—something I believe Sakamoto-sensei, who
explored the intersection of music and technology throughout his career, might have
appreciated.

== What I am Asking ==

I would be honored if the Sakamoto Foundation could review this work. I am happy to
share:

- The full research report (HTML and PDF)
- Generated audio samples (MIDI and rendered piano WAV)
- A short video explaining the methodology
- The complete source code (open source on GitHub)
- A Tonnetz visualization animation showing how "hibari"'s notes light up across
  the harmonic lattice

If the foundation finds this work meaningful, I would be deeply grateful for any
form of acknowledgment, sharing, or feedback. My goal is not commercial—I simply
want to express, through this research, how Sakamoto-sensei's music continues to
inspire scholarly inquiry in unexpected fields.

GitHub repository: https://github.com/doobMM/hibari_tda

Thank you sincerely for your time and for preserving the legacy of one of the most
important musicians of our time.

With deep respect,

Min-ju Kim
[your email]
[your affiliation, if any]
```

## 3. 일본어 버전 (간략)

```
坂本龍一財団 御中

韓国の学生のキム・ミンジュと申します。坂本龍一先生の「hibari」を深く愛する者として、

分析する研究を行いました。

「hibari」のミニマルな反復構造が、なぜこれほど豊かな感情を呼び起こすのか。
その答えを数学に求め、楽曲に隠された循環構造（cycle）を抽出し、その構造を保存
しながら新しい音楽を生成するパイプラインを構築しました。

・Tonnetz（音楽理論における和声格子）に基づく距離関数を導入し、原曲との構造的
類似度を従来手法の18倍まで向上させました
・「hibari」の4小節モジュール構造を尊重した深層学習により、Jensen-Shannon
ダイバージェンス0.002という高い再現度を達成しました

研究の方法論と音楽的解釈は私が設計し、コード実装はAnthropic社のClaude AIを
ツールとして使用しました。

上げました。坂本先生の音楽が学術研究にもたらす影響力を、財団と共有できれば
幸いです。

ご高覧いただけますと幸いです。

敬具
キム・ミンジュ
```
