# Residue Studies

A design philosophy for objects that survive inquiry.

---

**On presence and trace.** Every mark left on a surface is evidence of attention — not conclusion. The lines here do not explain; they remain. They exist at the threshold between precision and gesture, between what can be measured and what can only be felt. The hand that made them was not trying to convince anyone of anything. It was trying not to look away.

**On form and emptiness.** Space is not absence. The open palm holds nothing and therefore holds everything that might be placed into it. The void at the center of a geometric figure is the reason the figure exists at all. Negative space is drawn with the same care as the mark itself — painstaking attention to where the line is *not* is what gives the line meaning. Every composition is anchored by at least one silence.

**On lineweight and confidence.** Not all marks carry equal conviction. A line drawn without hesitation is a different thing from a line that corrects itself mid-stroke. Both are honest. The first voice proceeds; the second voice wobbles and adjusts and eventually arrives. The difference between them is information, not error. This philosophy treats variation in weight as a kind of notation — a score for attention.

**On the economy of sequence.** Visual progression moves from least to most: one element becomes two, two become a surface, a surface becomes a volume. The diagram does not explain this movement; it enacts it. The eye follows the logic before the mind does. Text, where it appears at all, is a whisper — a single character suspended in white space, acknowledging that the shape already said what needed to be said.

**On material honesty.** The work should look like it was made by a person sitting alone with a pencil and a piece of cream paper and enough time. Not perfect — exact. The imperfections are load-bearing. They prove that the mark was made in real time, by a hand that cared. The result of this kind of attention is not polish; it is sincerity.
