Surprises & Key Findings

r = -0.88 num_chords vs best_js — More chords = lower JS divergence. The richer the harmonic vocabulary, the better PH can reconstruct. The Last Emperor (272 chords, JS 0.029) vs a flower is not a flower (133 chords, JS 0.099).
r = -0.95 N (unique notes) vs best_js — Strongly negative. More unique notes = dramatically better generation. This is the single strongest predictor of pipeline quality.
r = +0.82 tonnetz_improvement vs T (length) — Longer pieces see bigger Tonnetz degradation vs frequency. Tonnetz advantage is specific to short, harmonically rich pieces (hibari). Longer pieces favor frequency.
Counter-intuitive All 6 tracks: frequency wins — Despite hibari's famous Tonnetz advantage (-47%), for all other tracks frequency is the best metric. The "Tonnetz is always better" assumption does not generalize.

Surprises & Key Findings

r = +0.98 pitch_js vs transition_js (vs_original) — Near-perfect correlation. If pitch distribution diverges, so does transition structure. They measure overlapping aspects of "structural distance."
r = +0.96 pitch_js vs dtw (vs_original) — DTW (temporal alignment) is almost perfectly predicted by pitch divergence. Sequence-level and distribution-level quality are tightly coupled.
Decoupled val_loss vs pitch_js (vs_ref): r = -0.38 — Lower validation loss does NOT predict better pitch fidelity to reference. The network optimizes something different from what our evaluation metrics capture.
Split pattern orig vs major clusters — The data clearly splits into two clusters. "major" experiments have ~10x higher pitch_js vs_original but similar pitch_js vs_ref. The scale change is correctly captured as "different from original" but "internally coherent."

Surprises & Key Findings

r = +0.94 js_mean vs kl_mean — JS and KL divergence are nearly redundant. Reporting both adds little information; one suffices for the committee.
r = -0.82 n_cycles vs js_mean — More PH cycles = better generation quality. The pipeline fundamentally benefits from richer topological structure. This validates the core thesis.
r = -0.71 n_notes vs js_mean — More generated notes correlates with lower JS, but weaker than n_cycles. Topology matters more than volume.
Outlier Improvement F (continuous + FC) — JS 0.0004, an order of magnitude better than everything else. This single point pulls the continuous overlap regression line dramatically. It's real, but the committee should know it's from a specialized FC architecture, not a general result.
